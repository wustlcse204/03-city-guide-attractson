London City Guide to College Graduates

One of the most vibrant cities in the world, London is the greatest city for college graduates to visit. No matter what you are interested in, you can experience whatever you want to in London, from romantic boat ride from Big Ben to 'pubbing' in the Strand. I have lived in London for several months, and I want to make few recommendations where young college Graduates should visit.

Thames River

I will tell tell you secret route that I walked when my friends visited London. It starts from the Big Ben and end at Tower Bridge: Big Ben to South Bank; walk along South Bank until the Tate Modern; cross the bridge to do walk on the northern side or keep walking along the south part till london bridge. Simply the best route ever. I recommend visiting Tate Modern.

Soccer

Of course you need to watch soccer in the U.K. There are six EPL football clubs in London (Arsenal, Chelsea, Crystal Palace, Fulham, Tottenham Hotspur, and West Ham United). I visited Emirates Stadium to see North London Derby. Atmosphere was hectic; the stadium was filled with fans cheering for their team while mocking the opponent. I recommend visiting any of these three stadiums.

  1. Stanford Bridge (Chelsea) : Fulham Rd, Fulham, London SW6 1HS, UK
  2. Emirates Stadium (Arsenal) Hornsey Rd, London N7 7AJ, UK
  3. White Hart Lane (Tottenham Spurs) 748 High Rd, Tottenham, London N17 0AP, UK
